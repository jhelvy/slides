China’s vehicle production and sales volumes increase 29.2% and 23.5% y/y in December, respectively
On January 11, the China Association of Automobile Manufacturers (CAAM) announced China’s vehicle production and sales results for December 2023 and the whole of 2023.
In 2023, China’s auto production and sales volumes hit new record highs, with two-digit growth year-over-year (y/y). Decent growth in the passenger vehicle (PV) market continued. Commercial vehicle (CV) volumes recovered to four million units. New Energy Vehicle (NEV) volumes maintained rapid growth and exceeded nine million units, with a market share of over 30%. Vehicle export volume totaled nearly five million units, registering a new high and effectively driving the industry’s overall rapid growth.
The CAAM expects that in 2024, China’s auto production and sales volumes will remain growing in a stable manner and achieve a more than 3% increase.
In December, vehicle production and sales volumes totaled 3.079 million units and 3.156 million units, up 29.2% and 23.5% y/y, respectively. Year-to-date (YTD) production and sales volumes were 30.161 million units and 30.094 million units, up 11.6% and 12% y/y, respectively.
For PVs, production and sales volumes in December totaled 2.713 million units and 2.792 million units, up 27.7% y/y and 23.3% y/y, respectively. YTD production and sales volumes totaled 26.124 million units and 26.063 million units, up 9.6% and 10.6% y/y, respectively.
For CVs, production and sales volumes in December totaled 366,000 units and 364,000 units, up 41.7% and 25.1% y/y, respectively. YTD production and sales volumes were 4.037 million units and 4.031 million units, up 26.8% and 22.1%, respectively.
For NEVs, production and sales volumes in December totaled 1.172 million units and 1.191 million units, up 47.5% and 46.4% y/y, respectively. Specifically, BEV (battery electric vehicle) production and sales volumes totaled 811,000 units and 825,000 units, up 35.2% and 32.2% y/y, respectively; PHV (plug-in hybrid vehicle) production and sales volumes totaled 360,000 units and 364,000 units, up 85.3% and 93% y/y, respectively; FCV (fuel cell vehicle) production and sales volumes totaled 1,000 units and 2,000 units, up 98.8% y/y and 149.1% y/y, respectively.
YTD NEV production and sales volumes totaled 9.587 million units and 9.495 million units, up 35.8% and 37.9% y/y, respectively. Specifically, BEV production and sales volumes totaled 6.704 million units and 6.685 million units, up 22.6% and 24.6% y/y, respectively; PHV production and sales volumes totaled 2.877 million units and 2.804 million units, up 81.2% and 84.7% y/y, respectively; FCV production and sales volumes both totaled 6,000 units, up 55.3% and 72% y/y, respectively.
In December, vehicle exports totaled 499,000 units, up 54.3% y/y. Specifically, PV exports totaled 422,000 units, up 53.3% y/y; CV exports totaled 78,000 units, up 59.9% y/y; NEV exports totaled 111,000 units, up 36.5% y/y. YTD vehicle exports totaled 4.91 million units, up 57.9% y/y. Specifically, PV exports totaled 4.14 million units, up 63.7% y/y; CV exports totaled 770,000 units, up 32.2% y/y; NEV exports totaled 1.203 million units, up 77.6% y/y.

China’s vehicle production and sales volumes increase 29.4% and 27.4% y/y in November, respectively
On December 11, the China Association of Automobile Manufacturers (CAAM) announced China’s vehicle production and sales results for November 2023.
In November, China’s auto market performance kept improving and turned out better than expected as demand for vehicle purchase was further released in the context of stimuli for auto consumption such as the Double 11 shopping festival, with the monthly vehicle production and sales volumes both growing year-over-year (y/y) and month-over-month. Passenger vehicle (PV) production and sales volumes remained good, and commercial vehicle (CV) production and sales volumes maintained rapid growth. New Energy Vehicle (NEV) and vehicle exports led the total export growth.
With preferential policies taking effect, regional motor shows and promotions increasing, and automakers making all-out efforts in hitting their annual sales targets at the end of the year, the December auto market is expected to further strengthen and see a year-end tail-raising effect once again. The vehicle production and sales volumes for the year are likely to hit new highs and beat the estimated annual targets.
In November, vehicle production and sales volumes totaled 3.093 million units and 2.97 million units, up 29.4% and 27.4% y/y, respectively. Year-to-date (YTD) production and sales volumes were 27.111 million units and 26.938 million units, up 10% and 10.8% y/y, respectively.
For PVs, production and sales volumes in November totaled 2.705 million units and 2.604 million units, up 25.6% y/y and 25.3% y/y, respectively. YTD production and sales volumes totaled 23.441 million units and 23.272 million units, up 8% and 9.3% y/y, respectively.
For CVs, production and sales volumes in November totaled 388,000 units and 366,000 units, up 64.9% and 44.6% y/y, respectively. YTD production and sales volumes were 3.671 million units and 3.666 million units, up 25.4% and 21.8%, respectively.
For NEVs, production and sales volumes in November totaled 1.074 million units and 1.026 million units, up 39.2% and 30% y/y, respectively. Specifically, BEV (battery electric vehicle) production and sales volumes totaled 727,000 units and 702,000 units, up 22.7% and 13.5% y/y, respectively; PHV (plug-in hybrid vehicle) production and sales volumes totaled 347,000 units and 323,000 units, up 93.7% and 89.5% y/y, respectively; FCV (fuel cell vehicle) production and sales volumes both totaled 1,000 units, up 123.9% y/y and 76.3% y/y, respectively.
YTD NEV production and sales volumes totaled 8.426 million units and 8.304 million units, up 34.5% and 36.7% y/y, respectively. Specifically, BEV production and sales volumes totaled 5.893 million units and 5.860 million units, up 21.1% and 23.6% y/y, respectively; PHV production and sales volumes totaled 2.528 million units and 2.439 million units, up 81.4% and 83.5% y/y, respectively; FCV production and sales volumes both totaled 4,000 units, up 48.9% and 56.9% y/y, respectively.
In November, vehicle exports totaled 482,000 units, up 46.3% y/y. Specifically, PV exports totaled 407,000 units, up 47% y/y; CV exports totaled 76,000 units, up 42.7% y/y; NEV exports totaled 97,000 units, up 1.6% y/y. YTD vehicle exports totaled 4.412 million units, up 58.4% y/y. Specifically, PV exports totaled 3.72 million units, up 65.1% y/y; CV exports totaled 692,000 units, up 29.8% y/y; NEV exports totaled 1.091 million units, up 83.5% y/y.

China’s vehicle production and sales volumes increase 11.2% and 13.8% y/y in October, respectively

In October, the holding of regional motor shows and promotion activities and the launch of new models greatly increased dealership traffic and further boosted auto consumption, with monthly sales volume largely flat month-over-month but notably up year-over-year (y/y). Passenger vehicle (PV) production and sales volumes remained good amid recovering auto consumption, and commercial vehicle (CV) production and sales volumes were lifted by factors such as a recovery in the logistics industry. New Energy Vehicle (NEV) and vehicle exports maintained rapid growth.
According to the CAAM, as national policies of stabilizing economic growth have been taking effect and measures such as regional subsidies and promotions for vehicle purchase have been continued, Q4 demand will continue to grow. The auto industry is therefore expected to achieve its annual sales targets successfully.
In October, vehicle production and sales volumes totaled 2.891 million units and 2.853 million units, up 11.2% and 13.8% y/y, respectively. Year-to-date (YTD) production and sales volumes were 24.016 million units and 23.967 million units, up 8% and 9.1% y/y, respectively.

Specifically, BEV (battery electric vehicle) production and sales volumes totaled 656,000 units and 646,000 units, up 12.4% and 18.8% y/y, respectively; PHV (plug-in hybrid vehicle) production and sales volumes totaled 332,000 units and 310,000 units, up 83.4% and 80.2% y/y, respectively; FCV (fuel cell vehicle) production and sales volumes totaled 400 units and 500 units, down 48.2% y/y and 56.4% y/y, respectively.

China vehicle production and sales decrease 34.3% and 35% in January, respectively
On February 10, the China Association of Automobile Manufacturers (CAAM) announced vehicle production and sales results for January 2023.
In January 2023, affected by the Spring Festival holiday and with consumer demand partially overdrawn, coupled with the expiration of purchase tax incentives for conventional ICE vehicles and subsidy policies for New Energy Vehicles (NEVs), the performance of the auto market was lackluster, with production and sales volumes showing double-digit declines in both month-over-month (m/m) and year-over-year (y/y) terms. Specifically, passenger vehicle production and sales volumes fell significantly; commercial vehicle production and sales volumes stayed at low levels; NEV production and sales volumes and auto exports remained good.
Looking ahead to Q1, the task of stabilizing the growth of China’s auto industry remains arduous and requires a sustained policy boost. Recently, government authorities have claimed that they will continue to stabilize and expand auto consumption, while many regions have successively introduced policies to promote auto consumption and encourage NEV development, which will bring confidence in the stable development of the auto market.
Overall vehicle production and sales in January totaled 1,594,000 units and 1,649,000 units, down 34.3% and 35% y/y, respectively.
In January, passenger vehicle production and sales reached 1,397,000 units and 1,469,000 units, both down 32.9% y/y.
For commercial vehicles, production in January totaled 197,000 units, reflecting a 43.1% y/y decrease. Sales were 180,000 units, reflecting a 47.7% y/y decrease.
In January, NEV production and sales reached 425,000 units and 408,000 units, down 6.9% and 6.3% y/y, respectively. Specifically, BEV (battery electric vehicle) production was 299,000 units, reflecting a 19.6% y/y decrease, while sales were 287,000 units, reflecting an 18.2% y/y decrease. PHV (plug-in hybrid vehicle) production was 126,000 units, reflecting a 48.5% y/y increase, while sales were 121,000 units, reflecting a 42.5% y/y increase. FCV (fuel cell vehicle) production and sales were 1,000 units and 2,000 units, down 54.2% and 15.6% y/y, respectively.
Overall vehicle exports in January reached 301,000 units, reflecting a 30.1% y/y increase. Specifically, passenger vehicle exports totaled 250,000 units, reflecting a 35.6% y/y increase. Commercial vehicle exports totaled 50,000 units, reflecting an 8.1% y/y increase. NEV exports totaled 83,000 units, reflecting an increase of 48.2% y/y.
