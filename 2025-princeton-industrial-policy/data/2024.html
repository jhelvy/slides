China’s December vehicle production, sales volumes increase 9.3% y/y, 10.5% y/y, respectively
On January 13, the China Association of Automobile Manufacturers (CAAM) announced China’s vehicle production and sales results for December 2024 and the whole of 2024.
In 2024, with the issuance of policies for large-scale equipment replacement and consumer goods trade-in, China’s automobile industry stepped up its transition and hit new production and sales highs, showing robust resilience and vitality for development and becoming a key driver for economic growth. The industry, however, is still facing a number of difficulties and challenges in economic operation, including mounting unfavorable factors from changes in the external environment, insufficient endogenous momentum for automobile consumption due to lack of consumer confidence, and a negative impact of intensifying industry competition and shrinking profits on sound and sustainable industry development.
According to the CAAM, China will adhere to the principle of pursuing progress while ensuring stability in terms of economic development in 2025, a move conducive to further strengthening development confidence, stimulating market vitality, and driving continuous economic upturn. On January 8, China’s National Development and Reform Commission and Ministry of Finance jointly issued the “Notification of Expanding the Implementation Scope of Policies for Large-scale Equipment Replacement and Consumer Goods Trade-in in 2025”. The policies have been taking a combined effect and will further release the potential of the automobile market. It is expected that in 2025, the market will continue to grow in a stable manner and see production and sales growth.
In December, vehicle production and sales volumes totaled 3.366 million units and 3.489 million units, up 9.3% y/y and 10.5% y/y, respectively. Year-to-date (YTD) production and sales volumes were 31.282 million units and 31.436 million units, up 3.7% y/y and 4.5% y/y, respectively.
For passenger vehicles (PVs), production and sales volumes in December totaled 3.006 million units and 3.12 million units, up 10.8% y/y and 11.7% y/y, respectively. YTD production and sales volumes totaled 27.477 million units and 27.563 million units, up 5.2% y/y and 5.8% y/y, respectively.
For commercial vehicles (CVs), production and sales volumes in December totaled 361,000 units and 369,000 units, down 1.5% y/y and up 1.2% y/y, respectively. YTD production and sales volumes were 3.805 million units and 3.873 million units, down 5.8% y/y and 3.9% y/y, respectively.
For New Energy Vehicles (NEVs), production and sales volumes in December totaled 1.53 million units and 1.596 million units, up 30.5% y/y and 34% y/y, respectively. Specifically, BEV (battery electric vehicle) production and sales volumes totaled 942,000 units and 973,000 units, up 16.1% y/y and 17.9% y/y, respectively; PHV (plug-in hybrid vehicle) production and sales volumes totaled 588,000 units and 622,000 units, up 63.4% y/y and 70.8% y/y, respectively; and FCV (fuel cell vehicle) production and sales volumes totaled 302 units and 268 units, down 76.7% y/y and 82.3% y/y, respectively.
YTD NEV production and sales volumes totaled 12.888 million units and 12.866 million units, up 34.4% y/y and 35.5% y/y, respectively. Specifically, BEV production and sales volumes totaled 7.758 million units and 7.719 million units, up 15.7% y/y and 15.5% y/y, respectively; PHV production and sales volumes totaled 5.125 million units and 5.141 million units, up 78.1% y/y and 83.3% y/y, respectively; and FCV production and sales volumes both totaled 5,000 units, down 10.4% y/y and 12.6% y/y, respectively.
In December, vehicle exports totaled 504,000 units, up 1.1% y/y. Specifically, PV exports totaled 426,000 units, up 1.1% y/y; CV exports totaled 78,000 units, up 1% y/y; and NEV exports totaled 134,000 units, up 19.8% y/y. YTD vehicle exports totaled 5.859 million units, up 19.3% y/y. Specifically, PV exports totaled 4.955 million units, up 19.7% y/y; CV exports totaled 904,000 units, up 17.5% y/y; and NEV exports totaled 1.284 million units, up 6.7% y/y.

China’s November vehicle production, sales volumes increase 11.1% y/y, 11.7% y/y, respectively
On December 11, the China Association of Automobile Manufacturers (CAAM) announced China’s vehicle production and sales results for November 2024.
In November, China’s vehicle market remained hot, as trade-in policies kept taking effect and vehicle purchase demand was further released. Vehicle production and sales volumes both continued to grow year-over-year (y/y) and month-over-month (m/m), setting monthly highs. The passenger vehicle (PV) market continued to strengthen. The commercial vehicle (CV) market, however, was relatively sluggish. The New Energy Vehicle (NEV) market maintained rapid growth, strongly supporting the overall market.
According to the CAAM, with the accumulative effect of policies emerging, local governments and automakers stepping up promotions, and dealers rushing for higher sales volumes at the end of the year, China’s vehicle market will strengthen further in December. It is expected that the vehicle production and sales volumes for the whole year will exceed 30 million units.
In November, vehicle production and sales volumes totaled 3.437 million units and 3.316 million units, up 11.1% y/y and 11.7% y/y, respectively. Year-to-date (YTD) production and sales volumes were 27.903 million units and 27.94 million units, up 2.9% y/y and 3.7% y/y, respectively.
For PVs, production and sales volumes in November totaled 3.109 million units and 3.001 million units, up 14.9% y/y and 15.2% y/y, respectively. YTD production and sales volumes totaled 24.459 million units and 24.435 million units, up 4.3% y/y and 5% y/y, respectively.
For CVs, production and sales volumes in November totaled 327,000 units and 315,000 units, down 15.7% y/y and 13.9% y/y, respectively. YTD production and sales volumes were 3.444 million units and 3.505 million units, down 6.2% y/y and 4.4% y/y, respectively.
For NEVs, production and sales volumes in November totaled 1.566 million units and 1.512 million units, up 45.8% y/y and 47.4% y/y, respectively. Specifically, BEV (battery electric vehicle) production and sales volumes totaled 943,000 units and 908,000 units, up 29.7% y/y and 29.3% y/y, respectively; PHV (plug-in hybrid vehicle) production and sales volumes totaled 623,000 units and 604,000 units, up 79.7% y/y and 86.8% y/y, respectively; and FCV (fuel cell vehicle) production and sales volumes totaled 393 units and 381 units, down 45.5% y/y and 41.1% y/y, respectively.
YTD NEV production and sales volumes totaled 11.345 million units and 11.262 million units, up 34.6% y/y and 35.6% y/y, respectively. Specifically, BEV production and sales volumes totaled 6.806 million units and 6.738 million units, up 15.5% y/y and 15.0% y/y, respectively; PHV production and sales volumes totaled 4.534 million units and 4.519 million units, up 79.4% y/y and 85.2% y/y, respectively; and FCV production and sales volumes both totaled 5,000 units, up 8.9% y/y and 11.7% y/y, respectively.
In November, vehicle exports totaled 490,000 units, up 1.6% y/y. Specifically, PV exports totaled 419,000 units, up 3.2% y/y; CV exports totaled 71,000 units, down 6.6% y/y; and NEV exports totaled 83,000 units, down 14.1% y/y. YTD vehicle exports totaled 5.345 million units, up 21.2% y/y. Specifically, PV exports totaled 4.52 million units, up 21.5% y/y; CV exports totaled 826,000 units, up 19.3% y/y; and NEV exports totaled 1.141 million units, up 4.5% y/y.

China’s October vehicle production, sales volumes increase 3.6% y/y, 7% y/y, respectively
On November 11, the China Association of Automobile Manufacturers (CAAM) announced China’s vehicle production and sales results for October 2024.
In October, with China’s automobile trade-in policies having a positive effect on automobile consumption, regional motor shows and price-off promotions in full swing, and automakers launching a large number of new models, China’s vehicle sales grew both year-over-year (y/y) and month-over-month (m/m). Amid bullish factors, the passenger vehicle (PV) market continued to strengthen. The commercial vehicle (CV) market, however, was relatively sluggish. New Energy Vehicle (NEV) and vehicle exports maintained rapid growth.
According to the CAAM, the facts that the accumulative effect of policies are emerging and that automakers and dealers are rushing for higher sales volumes at the end of the year will help continuously release the demand for automobile consumption, which could keep the upward momentum in China’s vehicle market going into the last two months of the year. Now that automobile trade-in policies have a remarkable boosting effect after being strengthened, the CAAM suggests that the policies be extended to 2025, and that the related implementation details be unveiled as early as possible.
In October, vehicle production and sales volumes totaled 2.996 million units and 3.053 million units, up 3.6% y/y and 7% y/y, respectively. Year-to-date (YTD) production and sales volumes were 24.466 million units and 24.624 million units, up 1.9% y/y and 2.7% y/y, respectively.
For PVs, production and sales volumes in October totaled 2.706 million units and 2.755 million units, up 7.7% y/y and 10.7% y/y, respectively. YTD production and sales volumes totaled 21.349 million units and 21.434 million units, up 3% y/y and 3.7% y/y, respectively.
For CVs, production and sales volumes in October totaled 290,000 units and 298,000 units, down 23.3% y/y and 18.3% y/y, respectively. YTD production and sales volumes were 3.117 million units and 3.19 million units, down 5.1% y/y and 3.4% y/y, respectively.
For NEVs, production and sales volumes in October totaled 1.463 million units and 1.43 million units, up 48% y/y and 49.6% y/y, respectively. Specifically, BEV (battery electric vehicle) production and sales volumes totaled 870,000 units and 842,000 units, up 32.6% y/y and 30.4% y/y, respectively; PHV (plug-in hybrid vehicle) production and sales volumes totaled 593,000 units and 587,000 units, up 78.4% y/y and 89.7% y/y, respectively; and FCV (fuel cell vehicle) production and sales volumes totaled 491 units and 563 units, up 19.2% y/y and 18.8% y/y, respectively.
YTD NEV production and sales volumes totaled 9.779 million units and 9.75 million units, up 33% y/y and 33.9% y/y, respectively. Specifically, BEV production and sales volumes totaled 5.863 million units and 5.83 million units, up 13.5% y/y and 13.0% y/y, respectively; PHV production and sales volumes totaled 3.911 million units and 3.915 million units, up 79.3% y/y and 85.0% y/y, respectively; and FCV production and sales volumes totaled 4,679 units and 4,695 units, up 25.9% y/y and 27.4% y/y, respectively.
In October, vehicle exports totaled 542,000 units, up 11.1% y/y. Specifically, PV exports totaled 465,000 units, up 10.7% y/y; CV exports totaled 76,000 units, up 13.6% y/y; and NEV exports totaled 128,000 units, up 3.6% y/y. YTD vehicle exports totaled 4.855 million units, up 23.8% y/y. Specifically, PV exports totaled 4.1 million units, up 24% y/y; CV exports totaled 755,000 units, up 22.5% y/y; and NEV exports totaled 1.058 million units, up 6.3% y/y.

In July, China’s overall automobile market entered the traditional off season and was relatively flat, with production and sales volumes dropping month-over-month (m/m) and year-over-year (y/y). Passenger vehicle (PV) and commercial vehicle (CV) production and sales volumes all shrank m/m and y/y, while New Energy Vehicle (NEV) and vehicle export volumes maintained rapid growth y/y.
According to the CAAM, two Chinese government bodies issued the “Several Measures to Strengthen Support for Large-scale Equipment Renewal and Consumer Goods Trade-in”, which provide much stronger support than the “Detailed Rules for the Implementation of Automobile Trade-in Subsidies” issued on April 24 and are applicable to both PVs and CVs. Such policies will further release replacement demand in the stock automobile market. Additionally, the continuous launch of new models and the introduction of vehicle purchase-related incentives from some regional governments will help achieve the expected goals for the year.
In July, vehicle production and sales volumes totaled 2.286 million units and 2.262 million units, down 4.8% y/y and 5.2% y/y, respectively. Year-to-date (YTD) production and sales volumes were 16.179 million units and 16.31 million units, up 3.4% y/y and 4.4% y/y, respectively.
For PVs, production and sales volumes in July totaled 2.03 million units and 1.994 million units, down 4% y/y and 5.1% y/y, respectively. YTD production and sales volumes totaled 13.917 million units and 13.974 million units, up 3.9% y/y and 4.5% y/y, respectively.
For CVs, production and sales volumes in July totaled 256,000 units and 268,000 units, down 10.5% y/y and 6.6% y/y, respectively. YTD production and sales volumes were 2.262 million units and 2.336 million units, up 0.4% y/y and 3.5% y/y, respectively.
For NEVs, production and sales volumes in July totaled 984,000 units and 991,000 units, up 22.3% y/y and 27% y/y, respectively. Specifically, BEV (battery electric vehicle) production and sales volumes totaled 569,000 units and 551,000 units, up 3.3% y/y and 2.6% y/y, respectively; PHV (plug-in hybrid vehicle) production and sales volumes totaled 415,000 units and 438,000 units, up 63.3% y/y and 80.7% y/y, respectively; and FCV (fuel cell vehicle) production and sales volumes both totaled 1,000 units, up 272% y/y and 181.2% y/y, respectively.
YTD NEV production and sales volumes totaled 5.914 million units and 5.934 million units, up 28.8% y/y and 31.1% y/y, respectively. Specifically, BEV production and sales volumes totaled 3.559 million units and 3.57 million units, up 8.4% y/y and 10.1% y/y, respectively; PHV production and sales volumes totaled 2.351 million units and 2.361 million units, up 80.1% y/y and 84.5% y/y, respectively; and FCV production and sales volumes both totaled 3,000 units, up 28.0% y/y and 25.5% y/y, respectively.
In July, vehicle exports totaled 469,000 units, up 19.6% y/y. Specifically, PV exports totaled 399,000 units, up 22.4% y/y; CV exports totaled 70,000 units, up 5.8% y/y; and NEV exports totaled 103,000 units, up 2.2% y/y. YTD vehicle exports totaled 3.262 million units, up 28.8% y/y. Specifically, PV exports totaled 2.738 million units, up 30.1% y/y; CV exports totaled 524,000 units, up 22.6% y/y; and NEV exports totaled 708,000 units, up 11.4% y/y.
